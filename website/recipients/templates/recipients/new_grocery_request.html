We will try our best to provide you with everything you request. However, THE GROCERY BUNDLES DEPEND ON SUPPLY AVAILABILITY. SOMETIMES YOU MIGHT NOT RECEIVE EVERYTHING YOU ASKED FOR BECAUSE OF AVAILABILITY OF SUPPLIES. Thanks for your understanding!

There are other resources you can reach out to if you need more GROCERIES AND FOOD:

  • The City of Toronto and the Red Cross deliver food hampers to seniors and others in need who can’t leave their homes and don’t have a support network. CALL: 1-833-204-9952, Monday-Friday, 8.30am-4.30pm. Quick registration, delivery next business day.
  • If you have resources to fund groceries but are unable to leave the house, the Good Neighbour Project is supporting grocery deliveries
  • Mom's Kitchen provides food for free, no questions asked.
  • You can also access 211 Central to find food banks and meal services available to Toronto residents. You can call or chat with them for information about specific services (call or text 211).
  • If you're a Black identifying person, you can visit Uplift Kitchen to receive cooked meals. Send them an email or contact them through Instagram.
  • If you want to access other resources, visit Family Service Toronto
